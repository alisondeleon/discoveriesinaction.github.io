Discoveries Online.

Doing developmental science using remote research

Over the last three years, our lab (Conceptual Development and Social Cognition Lab at NYU) has been developing Discoveries Online, a platform for doing unmoderated remote research with children. We are now opening up this platform for other developmental scientists to use.

On this website, we detail our strategies for conducting remote research and how to host a remote study using Discoveries Online, if you choose to use our platform. This website will continue to be updated with additional content, but currently includes everything that we think you need to get a study launched online.

In addition to this website, we provide more details on our experience conducting online research in our pre-print, Advancing developmental science via unmoderated remote research with children. This includes the the benefits of conducting unmoderated remote research, the types of studies we think can thrive from this medium of research, and detailed information about the samples we have been able to recruit online.

How our lab conducts remote research

Each of these guides provides the steps that our lab takes to design and run studies online. In many cases, we provide the exact language or materials that we use. Any of these materials are free for you to use for research purposes!

If you adapt these materials when developing your own online studies, all we ask is that you please cite our pre-print:
Rhodes, M., Rizzo, M. T., Foster-Hanson, E., Moty, K., Leshin, R., Wang, M., Benitez, J., & Ocampo, J. D. (2020). Advancing developmental science via unmoderated remote research with children. Pre-print.
